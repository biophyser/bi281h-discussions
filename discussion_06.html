Summary

  • Proteins collapse from disordered "unfolded" state to more ordered "folded" state
  • Collapse is driven by hydrophobic effect
    • It is favorable to miminize contact with water
    • The folded state has a surface area less than that of the unfolded state

Summary (continued)

  • Protein folding is driven by an increase in entropy:
    • Enthalpy of system remains roughly constant ($\Delta H \approx 0$)
    • Entropy goes down down when the chain folds ($\Delta S_{chain} > 0 $)
    • Increased entropy of water release offsets loss of chain entropy ($\Delta S_{water} + \Delta S_{chain} < 0$)
  • The specific fold is determined by making sure hydrogen bonds (and other interactions) are satisfied even when collapsed
