What are some important reasons for membranes to exist?

Cell membranes

  • Allow concentration of valuable biomolecules (overcome diffusion!)

  • Allow different chemistry in different parts of the cell

  • Allow selective control of material passage

What are some of the design features of membranes?

Membrane design features

  • Spontaneous assembly
  • Flexible
  • Divisible
  • Selectively permeable
  • Can "host" other molecules

Flexibility

Membrane composition alters membrane fluidity

Credit: smc.edu

Unsaturated bonds disrupt packing and increase fluidity

Cholesterol "buffers" fluidity

  • At low temperature, lipids pack into a solid (think wax). Cholesterol disrupts packing and increase fluidity.
  • At high temperature, lipids are highly dynamic (think melting jello). Rigid cholesterol stiffens the system and decreases fluidity.

Selective permeability: Only some molecules can pass through the bilayer

Which molecules would be able to pass through a bilayer?

  • $Na^{+}$ No.
  • $CO_{2}$ Yes.
  • A protein molecule No.
  • $H_{2}O$ Yes.

Summary:

  • Membranes define an "inside" to cells, concentrate biomolecules, and allow diverse chemistry in the cell
  • Membranes arise from a tradeoff between hyrdophobic and polar interactions for amphipathic phospholipids
  • Membranes spontaneously assemble, are flexible, are divisible, are selectively permeable, and can "host" other molecules
